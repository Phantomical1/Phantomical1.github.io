About Me

As a recent high school graduate with an anti-school mindset, going into college was a bit of a gamble. I didn't know exactly what I wanted to do, but I certainly wanted to find something. With a long while of thinking, I narrowed my options down to three things...

  • Aerospace Engineering... which is much too costly, money being a huge factor on what you can do in life as of now. Additionally, my scholarship applying game was rather lacking.
  • Computer Science... which took me a semester of doing to tap out on. I learnt a lot of great things that I plan to continue applying and learning as a side hobby and potential secondary flow of cash, as this may prove beenficial for. Ultimately I found myself losing interest in computers the more it became 'my future' and my entire focus in education. School is a great way to learn, but sometimes not everyone can learn everything in such an environment.
  • Psychology... which is what I'm more or less landing on at long last. It was the third of my options I had always considered, and unfortunately the last I'e come to try. Specifically, I'm taking the Behavioral Sciences program to eventually major in a psychology field. I find the study of the human mind to be very fascinating, and can see myself blending said studies with the knowledge I've developed in coding.

Class wise, I've taken an introductory Webdev class in HTML and CSS, as well as an introductory C# class. On top of this, a computer basics class has been taken. I will not be furthering my knowledge in any of these areas through classes, but intend to self-teach myself alongside my regular education in pursuit of a psychology degree.
